› for your average test accuracy are you evaluating 1 out of 10 categories or 1 out of 2 categories?


• 1 out of 2 categories.

  These Split-CIFAR10 runs use task-local binary heads:

  - task 0 evaluates classes (0,1)
  - task 1 evaluates (2,3)
  - ...
  - task 4 evaluates (8,9)

  So the reported per-task accuracy and the “average test accuracy” are binary accuracies within each task’s 2-class slice, not a global 10-way CIFAR10 accuracy.

  Chance level here is therefore about 0.50, not 0.10.

  That’s why results like:

  - task-0 final 0.5938
  - average test 0.4938

  mean the system is only slightly above or around binary chance on several tasks, which is weaker than it might look if interpreted as 10-way CIFAR10.
